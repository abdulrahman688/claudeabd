Our Story

From ancient Palmyra to modern Damascus

270 CE - Palmyra, Syria

Queen Zenobia: The Warrior Queen

In 270 CE, Zenobia became queen of the Palmyrene Empire in Syria. Known for her intelligence, beauty, and military prowess, she ruled one of the most powerful empires in the ancient world.

She challenged the mighty Roman Empire, expanded her kingdom from Egypt to Anatolia, and created a prosperous multicultural society. Zenobia was fluent in multiple languages, advised by philosophers, and led her armies into battle.

Though her empire eventually fell, her spirit of strength, independence, and resilience lives on. She remains a symbol of Syrian heritage and female empowerment.

1,000 Years of Tradition

Aleppo soap has been made the same way for over a thousand years. This UNESCO-recognized craft has survived empires, wars, and time itself.

  • Pure olive oil and laurel oil
  • Cold-process traditional method
  • Hand-cut and stamped
  • Aged 6-9 months naturally
  • No chemicals or additives

The Making Process

Ancient techniques, modern hands

Step 1: Ingredients

We source the finest olive oil and laurel oil from local Syrian producers. Only natural, pure ingredients—nothing artificial.

Step 2: Cooking

The oils are cooked for three days in large vats using the ancient cold-process method, with constant stirring and monitoring.

Step 3: Cutting

The mixture is poured onto large sheets, smoothed by hand, and cut into traditional rectangular bars using wooden tools.

Step 4: Stamping

Each bar is hand-stamped with our quality mark, just as artisans have done for centuries in Aleppo's ancient souks.

Step 5: Stacking

Bars are stacked in tall towers in cool, dry rooms. This allows air circulation for proper aging and maturation.

Step 6: Aging

The soap ages naturally for 6-9 months. During this time, it turns from green to gold and becomes milder and more luxurious.

Our Vision

Like Queen Zenobia who built an empire of strength and prosperity, we're building a community of empowered women. Every bar of soap is a small act of resistance against poverty, a celebration of heritage, and an investment in dignity.

We envision a future where every Syrian widow who wants to work can earn a living wage through traditional crafts. Where ancient skills are preserved. Where customers choose ethics and quality over mass production.
